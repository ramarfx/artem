Michaelangelo Buonarroti

Artworks
Battle of Cascina (1504)

The Battle of Cascina is a lost painting that was commissioned from Michelangelo by the Republic of Florence in 1504. The painting was intended to depict a famous battle that took place in 1364 between Florence and Pisa, in which the Florentine army emerged victorious.

Sistine Chapel Ceiling (1508)

Depicts various scenes from the Book of Genesis, the first book of the Bible, as well as other related figures and motifs. The centerpiece of the ceiling is the iconic "Creation of Adam," which shows God reaching out to touch the finger of Adam, symbolizing the moment when he gives him the spark of life.

The Last Judgment (1541)

Located on the altar wall of the Sistine Chapel in Vatican City, Rome. Completed in 1541, the painting depicts the second coming of Christ and the final judgment of all humanity.

Cappella Paolina

This painting depicts the moment when the apostle Paul, formerly known as Saul of Tarsus, was converted to Christianity. The painting is based on the biblical account of the event, which is recorded in the Book of Acts, Chapter 9.

St. Jerome In The Wilderness (1480)

The painting depicts Saint Jerome during his retreat to the Syrian desert. St Jerome kneels in a rocky landscape, gazing toward a crucifix.

Adoration of The Magi (1481)

One of Leonardo’s first commissions by the Augustinian monks of San Donato a Scopeto in Florence. Although Leonardo didn’t finish it and left for Milan, it is still considered a masterpiece and has been in the Uffizi since 1670

Virgins of The Rocks (1483)

This painting depicts the apocryphal legend of the meeting in the wilderness between the young John the Baptist and Jesus returning home from Egypt..

Lady With an Ermine (1489)

A portrait of Cecilia Gallerani who was the mistress of Ludovico Sforza, Duke of Milan. Leonardo was probaly in the service of the Duke when this was painted. The ermine is though to symbolize purity.
