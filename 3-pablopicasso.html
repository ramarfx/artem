Pablo Picasso

Paintings
Flowers in a Grey Jug (1908)

Depicts a still life scene with a bouquet of flowers in a grey jug and a wine glass with a spoon resting on the edge. The painting is an early example of Picasso's Cubism style that sought to break down traditional forms and perspectives into geometric shapes and planes.

Portrait of Fernarde (1909)

It depicts Picasso's lover and muse, Fernande Olivier, who was the subject of many of his paintings during this period.Fernande is depicted in profile, with her face divided into geometric planes and shapes.

Ma Jolie (1911)

The woman's features are abstracted and reduced to basic shapes, with overlapping and intersecting planes used to create a sense of dynamism and energy. The painting revolutionized the art world and helped to lay the foundations for much of the 20th-century art that followed.

Still Life with Chair Caning (1912)

This painting includes a painted imitation of a chair caning, as well as other found objects like a pipe and a newspaper, all depicted in fragmented forms and flattened space.

Guitar (1913)

This painting is part of a series of works that depict a guitar in fragmented and abstracted form. It represents Picasso's interest in exploring the potential of objects as subjects, rather than just the traditional subjects of painting like people or landscapes.

Still Life With Guitar (1921)

The guitar in the painting is depicted in fragmented and abstracted form, with its strings and body reduced to geometric shapes and planes. The background and foreground are similarly simplified, with objects like a glass and a bottle rendered in sharp angles and flattened forms.

Head of a Woman (1907)

The sculpture depicts a woman's head in an abstracted and stylized form, with a combination of sharp angles and curves that break with traditional representational art. "Head of a Woman" is an important example of how Picasso was influenced by African art and its simplified, abstracted forms.

The Three Musicians (1921)

The three figures are positioned side by side in a flat, shallow space, with their instruments arranged in front of them. Each figure is depicted in a different color scheme, with the shapes overlapping and intersecting to create a sense of depth and movement.

Still Life with Mandolin (1924)

Picasso spent the summer of 1924 in the village of Juan-les-Pins on the Côte d'Azur. It is a night scene - in the foreground, a fruit dish, wine bottle, and mandolin rest on a table.

Still Life with Chair Caning (1912)

This painting includes a painted imitation of a chair caning, as well as other found objects like a pipe and a newspaper, all depicted in fragmented forms and flattened space.

Guitar (1908)

The three figures are depicted side by side in a shallow space, with their faces and bodies reduced to a series of planes and angles. Each figure is composed of a different combination of shapes and colors, and their poses suggest movement and activity.

The Actor (1904)

It has been suggested that the identity of “The Actor” is from the famous Nouveau Cirque in Paris. The painting has been interpreted as the idea of performance and illusion, with the actor serving as a symbol for the idea of artificiality and the mask that people wear in everyday life.

Garçon à la pipe (1905)

The painting depicts a young boy wearing a wreath of flowers and holding a pipe in his left hand. The boy's attire is a mix of urban and rural styles, with a blue-and-white striped shirt and a garland of flowers around his neck.

Two Women Running on the Beach (1922)

The painting has been interpreted as a celebration of the freedom and a reflection of Picasso's fascination with the beauty and power of the female form. The beach setting has also been interpreted as a reference to the artist's Spanish heritage and his love of the sea.

Bouquet of Peace (1958)

This painting shows his desire for people to join together in love and harmony. The brightly colored flowers convey a sense of hope and rebirth, and the bouquet forms a bond between two individuals symbolized by the two hands displayed within the piece.

Mother and Child (1901)

This painting has been interpreted in a number of ways, with some seeing it as a poignant representation of the bond between a mother and her child, while others view it as a commentary on the hardships and difficulties of motherhood, particularly in the face of poverty and deprivation.

Woman with Folded Arms (1901)

This painting has been interpreted as of the loneliness and isolation that many people experience, while some view it as a commentary on the position of women in society, particularly during the early 20th century.

The Old Guitarist (1903)

The painting reﬂects the twenty-two-year-old Picasso's personal struggle and sympathy for the plight of the downtrodden; he knew what it was like to be poor, having been nearly penniless during all of 1902.

Blind Man's Meal (1903)

The blind man is portrayed as helpless and vulnerable, with his head tilted down and his eyes closed. The piece of bread in his hand is a symbol of his struggle to survive and the meager existence that he leads.

The Tragedy (1903)

Picasso chose to depict forms of human loss and suffering in this painting. He chose to emulate nature in The Tragedy. Decent art resonates with our own human nature and the world in general. Moreover, it points out our personal weakness, personal failure and moral evil without shying away.
